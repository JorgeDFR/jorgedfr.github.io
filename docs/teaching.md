# Teaching

## Intelligent Robotics (MSc in EIC @ FEUP)

This course focused on introducing fundamental concepts in robotics, with a strong emphasis on mobile robotics and intelligent autonomous systems. The course introduced fundamental concepts such as robotic architectures, sensors and actuators, localization and mapping, motion planning and navigation, learning methods applied to robotics, and humanâ€“robot interaction. Moreover, the practical component of the course was composed by two group projects. The first project was common to all groups and aimed at introducing students to robotics software frameworks and algorithmic reasoning, through the exploration of the ROS framework and the Webots simulator to control a reactive two-wheel mobile robot using sensor data. The second project was proposed by each group and focused on the exploration of a specific component of an intelligent robotic system, such as mapping, localization, path planning, or path control, using either heuristic approaches or learning-based methods (e.g., reinforcement learning). In this project, students were also allowed to justify the use of alternative simulators (e.g., Webots, Gazebo, Isaac-Sim, etc.), communication mechanisms (e.g., ROS, ZMQ, etc.). In the final project the students have also the oportunity to work with real robots, using one of the following robots: Waveshare UGV Rover, Duckiebot, and Turtlebot 3 Waffle.

My participation in this course was mainly focused on project supervision in the practical classes, supporting students in the design, implementation, and validation of their robotic systems. Additionally, I developed a digital twin of the Waveshare UGV Rover robot in the Webots simulator and prepared supporting tutorials using ROS 2 and Webots, including practical examples of robotic algorithms such as the Adaptive Monte Carlo Localization (AMCL) algorithm.
